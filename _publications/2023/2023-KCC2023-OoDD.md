abstract: >-
  Out-of-distribution (OOD) detection is an essential capability for ensuring the reliability and stability of deep learning models at the deployment stage. Until recently, most research on OOD detection has relied predominantly on unimodal approaches. Lately, however, multimodal learning has attracted growing attention, as it is known to form a richer feature space than its unimodal counterpart. In this paper, we propose a method for improving the OOD detection performance of the Contrastive Language-Image Pre-training (CLIP) model, which jointly learns from two modalities—vision and language. Specifically, we perform contrastive fine-tuning using arbitrary text on a CLIP model that has been pre-trained on a large-scale dataset of internet image-text pairs. Through this work, we demonstrate the feasibility of multimodal approaches for out-of-distribution detection.
